About

I am a Computational Associate at the Xavier Lab in the Klarman Cell Observatory at The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. My research focuses on multi-modal single-cell and spatial transcriptomics for disease gene target discovery, with applications in atopic dermatitis, inflammatory bowel disease, and cancer.

I graduated Cum Laude from UC San Diego with a B.S. in Biology (Bioinformatics specialization), maintaining a 3.93 GPA. My work spans developing computational pipelines for large-scale genomic data analysis, spatial omics applications, and identifying therapeutic targets for immune-related diseases.
