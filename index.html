Tanvi Jain's Home Page

I am a Computational Associate at the Xavier Lab in the Klarman Cell Observatory at The Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, where I work on multi-modal single-cell and spatial transcriptomics for disease gene target discovery. My research focuses on applications in atopic dermatitis, inflammatory bowel disease, and cancer.

I graduated Cum Laude from UC San Diego with a B.S. in Biology (Bioinformatics specialization), maintaining a 3.93 GPA. Prior to my current position, I worked as a Lab Technician at the Salk Institute of Biological Sciences under Dr. Joseph Ecker, where I developed computational pipelines for large-scale genomic data analysis.

Field of Interests:

  • Spatial omics and spatial transcriptomics
  • Disease gene target discovery
  • Computational biology and bioinformatics
  • Immune-related diseases
